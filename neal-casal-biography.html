Neal’s career

  • Neal began working on early solo shows from 1990 to 1993 in studios in Los Angeles, New York and New Jersey.
  • There, he joined his manager Gary Waldman.
  • After signing an editorial agreement with Warner / Chappell Music, he forged a long-standing professional relationship with producer / engineer Jim Scott.
  • He then signed with Zoo Entertainment and recorded his debut album at the Palacio del Río in 1994.
  • He released Fade Away Diamond Time in September 1994 that was critically acclaimed and supported by a tour of the United States with his band.
  • He separated from Zoo Records in 1996 and recorded Rain, Wind and Speed ​​released by Buy or Die Records.
  • He signed with the Glitterhouse Records label and released five albums, including Field Recordings and The Sun Rises Here in 1997.
  • He released the self-produced Basement Dreams album, called American Album of the Year in Mojo magazine in 1998.
  • He partnered with Six String Drag leader Kenny Roby and toured Europe in 1999.
  • He released his sixth solo album Anytime Tomorrow in 2000.
  • Anytime Tomorrow was the last album released by Glitterhouse in 2000 and caused an extensive European tour in early 2001.
  • He co-wrote, produced and released the Ran On Pure Lightning EP in 2002.
  • The Railroad Earth 2002 album Bird in a House presented a version of Casal’s song “Dandelion Wine”.
  • All Directions, a compilation album of live and unpublished songs, was released in 2007.
  • He began recording his tenth solo album in March 2010.
  • He toured Europe, Fargo relaunched the albums Basement Dreams and Rain, Wind and Speed.
