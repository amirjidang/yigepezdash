Here's How Many Girlfriends Seinfeld Actually Dated On The Show

Highlights

  • Jerry Seinfeld had 73 girlfriends throughout the series, averaging a new girlfriend every two to three episodes.
  • Most of Jerry's relationships on "Seinfeld" were short-lived, with many lasting only a single episode.
  • The longest relationship Jerry had on the show was with his girlfriend Rachel Goldstein, played by Melanie Smith, who appeared in four different episodes.

With a show that's literally named after the lead actor, it was always clear who the real star of Seinfeld was. Jerry Seinfeld and his fellow cast members, including the sometimes-actor, part-producer, and part-writer Larry David, pulled from their own lives to inspire everything from the characters to the plotlines to the epic quotes.

One thing that wasn't exactly inspired by Jerry's real life? The comically long list of girlfriends sitcom Jerry had throughout the show. While Jerry was dating during the series, his relationship drama was fairly limited to the time he dated a 17-year-old and then the time his now-wife left her new husband for him. But sitcom Jerry enjoyed tons of different relationships, though ultimately, none of them worked out.

Jerry Had Many Notable Celebrity Girlfriends On Seinfeld

Over the course of Seinfeld's many episodes, Jerry Seinfeld had tons of love interests. Many of the episodes were centered on his relationship exploits, which didn't always end well. And while his co-stars got their share of romantic subplots, things often centered on Jerry.

Of course, things didn't usually go well for Jerry. Thanks to mix-ups, lies, and misunderstandings, plenty of his relationships ended poorly on screen.

Sometimes, the relationships ended inexplicably; Jerry even broke up with the perfect girlfriend, Marcia Brady. He also notably ruffled Teri Hatcher, whose resulting mic-drop moment made her even more famous.

Even Jerry's most iconic girlfriends, like award-winning Marlee Matlin, only last a single episode. So how many girlfriends did Jerry have on Seinfeld? A lot.

How Many Different Girlfriends Did Seinfeld Have On The Show?

Based on information from the DVD release of Seinfeld, Jerry had at least 73 girlfriends throughout the series. Not all of them appeared on-screen (some were only alluded to), and most of Jerry's relationship with Elaine was pre-series.

It's not clear if Elaine is included in that figure, either, but because many single-episode off-screen girlfriends are also included in the count, it can be assumed Elaine is, too. Elaine is also romantically involved with Jerry in between his other relationships, so she could 'count' more than one time in that sense.

But with 73 girlfriends over the course of the show's 180 episodes, Jerry has a new girlfriend, on average, about every two to three episodes.

How Many Of Seinfeld's Girlfriends Lasted More Than One Episode?

A handful of Jerry's relationships last longer than one episode on Seinfeld. Apart from Elaine, Jerry also dates Vanessa, Marla (Jane Leeves), Tia, Dolores, Rachel, and Jeannie (Janeane Garafalo) for multiple episodes.

In most cases, however, Jerry only dates his "girlfriends" for a single episode. Even his longer relationships spanned about two episodes on average.

Who Was Seinfeld's Longest Relationship With On The Show?

Most of Jerry's relationships in Seinfeld were short-lived and many lasted only a single episode. And sure, it made sense for bigger celebrities to only appear once to create buzz both for the show and their careers, but even lesser-known (at the time) characters would ride off into the sunset.

One single girlfriend of Jerry's appeared in four different episodes, making her the longest relationship Jerry ever had on screen. That girlfriend was "Rachel Goldstein," played by Melanie Smith.

Melanie appeared in three different episodes, one of them a two-parter. Rachel entered the series during season five, episode 18, and appeared in "The Raincoats" (the two-episode series), "The Hamptons," and "The Opposite."

Ultimately, Rachel dumps Jerry and he doesn't really mind. Why would he, though, when in the next season's first episode, he gets to date a Miss America contestant? Marguerite MacIntyre played Karen for a single episode, but it wasn't terrible for her career; she went on to enjoy various TV gigs, including long-running roles on both Kyle XY and The Vampire Diaries.

Melanie Smith enjoyed a similar trajectory; even before Seinfeld, she enjoyed a long run on As the World Turns, then later appeared on various TV shows like Star Trek: Deep Space Nine and The Division.

As for the central cast of Seinfeld, all have gone on to enjoy plenty of success, although none have been quite as lucky as Jerry in terms of wealth.
