These Are The Best-Selling Musicians' Biggest Album Flops

Highlights

  • Best-selling musicians sometimes experience album flops; negative reviews and poor sales don't always reflect their overall success.
  • Queen's Hot Space and Katy Perry's Smile were highly anticipated albums that failed to meet sales expectations.
  • Factors like experimental styles, controversial distribution methods, and personal challenges can contribute to disappointing album performances for popular musicians.

Music is a divine thing, and to achieve divinity, the best-selling musicians endeavor to create it precisely, in order to hit the right notes and enchant the audience.

Despite years of striving and extensive experience delighting fans, even the most popular musicians sometimes fail miserably ... but their reputation and ability to enrapture the crowd are a saving grace.

There are many examples where, despite negative reviews and poor critical ratings, a best-selling musician's album received millions in sales.

By the same token, there are some rare instances when legendary musicians failed miserably, and even their biggest fans couldn't prevent an album from becoming a gigantic financial failure, or "flop."

Let’s explore instances where wildly popular and acclaimed musicians surprisingly fell short of expectations, releasing albums that fell flat on the charts, unable to manage substantial sales.

8 Queen: Hot Space (1982)

More than eight hundred thousand units sold is a significant achievement for emerging artists, but this is Queen, one of the greatest bands in the history of rock.

One year before Hot Space was issued, Queen released compilations of their hit songs album under the name Greatest Hits, which sold 25 million copies.

Additionally, their album, The Game (1980) garnered twelve million copies sold.

Sadly, Freddie Mercury left the world in 1991, at the relatively young age of 45.

In their prime, they were selling millions, but an unexpected event occurred.

Hot Space couldn't sell a million copies, becoming the most disappointing album in Queen's career.

The tenth studio album from this great band was a kind of experimental album, and people rejected their pop dance version of the album.

6 U2: Songs of Innocence (2014)

Irish rock band U2 is among the greatest and most acclaimed modern bands in the world.

U2 was formed way back in 1976, and is still intact after nearly half a century.

U2 has sold 175 million copies worldwide, accounting for all of their releases. Their albums are known to create havoc in the musical world, and are typically met with eager anticipation.

The album Joshua Tree sold 25 million copies, while Achtung Baby and All That You Can't Leave Behind sold 18 and twelve million copies respectively.

The collective net worth of U2 is about $1.85 billion.

Larry Mullen Jr. has a net worth of $350 million, while Adam Clayton and the Edge each have an estimated net worth of $400 million.

U2's mononymous lead singer Bono has a net worth of $700 million.

U2 routinely sold millions of albums, then they released Songs Of Innocence; U2 decided to try something new when they teamed up with Apple to promote their album.

Famously, Apple released Songs Of Innocence via iTunes, in what would unfortunately come to be known as the "U2 iTunes controversy."

In late 2022, Bono again apologized for the manner in which the album was distributed.

Variety described the center of the controversy – that U2's Songs Of Innocence was added to the iTunes libraries of all users – and quoted Bono as he looked back on the disastrous incident:

What ensued shortly after was a controversy that many iTunes users can recall, with customers discovering the album on their phone, with no explanation as to how they’d received it.

"As one social media wisecracker put it, ‘Woke up this morning to find Bono in my kitchen, drinking my coffee, wearing my dressing gown, reading my paper.’ Or, less kind, ‘The free U2 album is overpriced.'

"Mea Culpa," Bono said of the reaction at the time. “If just getting our music to people who like our music was the idea, that was a good idea. But if the idea was getting our music to people who might not have had a remote interest in our music, maybe there might be some pushback.

"At first I thought this was just an internet squall, but quickly realized we’d bumped into a serious discussion about big tech ... I take full responsibility. Not Guy O, not Edge, not Adam, not Larry, not Tim Cook, not Eddy Cue. I’d thought if we could just put our music within reach of people, they might choose to reach out toward it. Not quite."

Despite some well liked songs, fans felt that Songs Of Innocence was forced upon half a billion people, and they quickly turned their ire toward the album itself, the band, and Apple.

Music pundits ultimately believed U2's Songs Of Innocence flopped because of Apple's unorthodox means of distributing it.

The top-selling female artist in country music, Shania Twain, released her self-titled album and sold more than a million copies.

However, her second album The Woman In Me led to her superstar status, by attracting 20 million album buyers.

Major success occurred when her album Come On Over broke all records and became the eighth highest-selling album of all time, selling an astonishing 40 million copies.

The princess of pop, Britney Spears, is one of the greatest pop singers of the 1990s and 2000s, managing to stand out in what was a golden age in the genre.

With more than 175 million sales, from 1999 to 2003, she created four albums and sold 60 million copies.

Forbescrowned Spears as the "Most Powerful Celebrity" in 2002.

She couldn't increase her net worth because, from 2008 through 2021, her father controlled her finances and life due to a controversial conservatorship.

Unfortunately, Britney Spears' rapidly ascendant musical career took an abrupt downturn after she shaved her head and appeared to have a high-profile breakdown in the mid-2000s.

A conservatorship controversy arose after her father filed a petition to seize control of her career, citing her alleged instability.

Against her will, her father Jamie Spears and an attorney became her conservators, gaining legal control over every aspect of her life for 13 years.

Over time, fans began investigating, and Spears' plight became more well known, leading to what was known as the "Free Britney Movement," a collective effort to draw attention to and end the strict conservatorship.

The modern musical wizardess, the Queen of Camp, Katy Perry, faced disappointment in the relatively recent year of 2020.

People anticipated that the singer of the song "Roar" would easily sell a million copies in a week.

Her album dropped after three years of waiting, with fans eagerly awaiting her new album.

Katy Perry released her album Smile in 2020, and in the first week, she only managed to sell 50,000 copies.

Critics and fans completely rejected her sixth studio album, rendering harsh criticism, such as "nonsensical lyrics" or "vulgar songs."

Despite this, fans continue to support her – but by many metrics, it appears Katy Perry's musical popularity is declining.

Some have speculated Perry's political activism has adversely impacted her career, while others suggested she lost interest in music because she is so engrossed in making money through her TV appearances.

Finally, a gentle reminder: Despite having millions of followers worldwide and numerous chart-topping songs in their collection, even the world's best-selling musicians can fail.

Musicians strive hard. Sometimes their efforts enchant the world, but at other times, they face disappointment, poor sales, and harsh criticism.

There is no certain formula or secret behind best-selling musicians' hits, as the finicky public has rejected albums for being too ambitious as well as for innovating too little.

However, the best-selling musicians tend to overcome an album flop, soon returning to make people dance and be inspired through their talent and art, as all of the above have done.
