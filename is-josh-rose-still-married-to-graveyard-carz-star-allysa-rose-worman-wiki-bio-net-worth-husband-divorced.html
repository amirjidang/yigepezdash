Graveyard Carz

“Graveyard Carz” is a reality television show which began airing in 2011, and follows the workshop Graveyard Carz as they focus on restoring Mopar muscle cars from the 1960s to the 1970s. The show has been running for a total of 10 seasons, and has the motto “It’s Mopar or No Car”. The show was created by Mark Worman using an independent film and television production company he created, called The Division. They marketed the concept of the show to the network Velocity, and their deal allowed them all creative control with a bit of input from the Velocity team.

The show has been known for its significant restorations, and departs from the usual nature of these types of series which show complete builds per episode, as they admit that it often takes a long time for them to complete a project. One of the most significant completions seen on the show was the restoration of a 1971 Plymouth Cuda, which many believed could not be restored, except for Worman; the car underwent a significant transformation during the first five seasons. The show also focuses on the cast members, known as The Ghouls. The series has shown a bit of insight into the cast members’ lives, but mostly it focuses more on the cars, as viewers comments indicate that they are mainly watching for the car builds.

Ex-Wife Alyssa Rose

She read a lot of auto magazines and often spent time in the garage where his father worked. She also pursued other hobbies growing up, including ballroom dancing, jazz, and ballet which she did throughout her teenage years. She attended summer workshops, and learned other types of dances in varying capacities.

She mainly stayed in the background, but was a recurring cast member for most of the early seasons of “Graveyard Carz”, and became a main cast member since the fifth season. She started training alongside the crew, and is now one of the more experienced workers in the garage. Most episodes see her helping get work done in the shop, and she has also been a part of a few comedic sequences. The production team makes it a point not to script any moment in the shop, so that everything is filmed in its natural state.
