Who Is Alex Wolff?

According to Rotten Tomatoes, Alex Wolff, a skilled American actor and musician, transitioned from his early days as a child star to a thriving adult acting career. Born in New York City to actress Polly Draper and jazz pianist Michael Wolff, he began showcasing his talents at a young age.

His mother created a feature-length pilot for The Naked Brothers Band: The Movie in 2005, a project that starred his older brother, Nat Wolff. This venture evolved into a cable show titled The Naked Brothers Band, which aired on Nickelodeon from 2007 to 2009, featuring the two brothers as musicians.

Wolff took on roles such as portraying a teenager searching for J.D. Salinger in Coming Through the Rye and depicting the real-life figure Dzhokhar Tsarnaev in Patriots Day. He lightened the mood with appearances in films like My Big Fat Greek Wedding 2 and My Friend Dahmer, where he starred alongside Ross Lynch.

Wolff's acting trajectory took a new turn when he was featured as the nerdy student Spencer Gilpin in the high-profile reboot Jumanji: Welcome to the Jungle. This successful journey continued in the sequel Jumanji: The Next Level, where he shared the screen with notable actors.

Diversifying his roles, Wolff garnered attention for his part in Ari Aster's horror film Hereditary and collaborated once more with his family for the indie film Stella's Last Weekend, playing a character in a romantic rivalry with his on-screen sibling.

Taking inspiration from his mother's multifaceted career, Wolff embarked on his directorial debut in The Cat and the Moon.
