Chris Iannetta is an American professional baseball player with an estimated net worth of $20 million.

Chris Iannetta started his career when he joined St. Raphael Academy baseball team. He received the All-State honors for three years and was also a recipient of the C. Dona Manyard Award. In college, he joined the University of North Carolina Tar Heels' baseball team. During his sophomore year with the team, he recorded .319 batting average and 55 RBI. He was named to the 2003 NCAA Starkville Regional All-Tournament Team and won the Defensive Player of the Year.

He was selected as a fourth round pick by the Colorado Rockies in the 2004 Major League Baseball Draft. Iannetta began his minor league stint with the Asheville Tourists. He played with other teams such as Modesto Nuts, Tulsa Drillers, and Colorado Springs Sky Sox.

Iannetta made his debut in the Majors on August 27, 2006. During his last season with the Rockies, he played in 112 games, batting .238 with 14 home runs and 55 RBI. He was traded to the Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim in November 2011.

Christopher Domenic Iannetta was born on April 8, 1983 in Providence, Rhode Island.
