Here's How Paris Hilton Earns &amp; Spends Her $300 Million Fortune

Known as the It Girl and famous for her phrases such as That’s Hot, Paris Hilton was born into a family fortune as an heiress to the Hilton Hotels. Her mother, Kathy, was a model, and young Paris made appearances at red carpet events. During her late teens, Hilton attended wild parties and became known for her lifestyle as a party girl who loved glitz and glamor. With her famous friends, wealthy lifestyle, and notable relationships, Paris Hilton was the talk of the town during her young years. Over time, she channeled her popularity into creating television shows and establishing a business empire.

From starring in shows and working as the world’s highest-paid DJ to spending her fortune on houses and big parties, Paris Hilton has an envious life with all the luxuries she can afford. Let’s look at how she earns and spends her $300 million net worth.

Earns: Reality Television Shows

In 2003, Paris Hilton had a reality television show with her then-friend Nicole Richie called The Simple Life that lasted for five seasons wherein the friends worked at simple jobs such as fast-food joint employees. According to Parade, it was reported that Hilton made $5 million per season for the show. She went on to star in various reality shows about her lifestyle and even made an appearance on Paradise Hotel in 2013, making $300,000 for four episodes.

Spends: Real Estate

Paris Hilton purchased a $5.9 million Mediterranean-style mansion in the Mulholland gated community in 2007, followed by a New York penthouse for $4.9 million in 2014. After getting engaged to now-husband Carter Reum, Hilton purchased a Malibu mansion in 2021 for $8.4 million. The three-bedroom, three-bathroom home was initially constructed in 1955 and renovated in the later years.

Earns: Music Career And An Author

Under the wing of Warner Bros. Records, Paris Hilton launched her record label Heiress Records and has released one studio album and nine singles to date. Her debut album Paris peaked at the sixth position on the Billboard 200 chart, and her single Stars Are Blind came to the No.18 spot on Billboard Hot 100. Alongside music, she has written two memoirs between 2004 and 2005. Her first book, Confessions of an Heiress: A Tongue-in-Cheek Peek Behind the Pose, was a New York Times bestseller.

Spends: Custom Dog Houses

Unsurprisingly, Paris Hilton treats her pets to the same luxurious life as herself. Her dogs live in a 3,000 sq. ft. home valued at $325,000. As mentioned by Elle Decor, it is a two-story Spanish-style mansion protected by iron gates for protection. Faye Resnick designed the house while the decor was shipped from Philippe Starck Furniture. The house comes with lightning, air-conditioning, heating, and a chandelier.

Earns: Business Venture And Appearances

Paris Hilton's most significant income source is her perfume empire which carries her name. Paris Hilton perfume was first launched in 2005 with a fruity floral scent called Paris Hilton by Paris Hilton. She has now introduced over twenty-eight fragrances for women and men and has become the second most successful perfume line at $3 billion after Elizabeth Taylor. As a successful reality star and businesswoman, Hilton charges $300,000 for making an appearance.

Spends: Private Aviation

While the heiress is yet to buy her private aviation, Hilton has always traveled through private jets to reach locations for her commitments and shows. She is often seen traveling in the Boeing 747 owned by Las Vegas Sands Corporation, valued at $270 million. The jet comes with leather seats and sofa-cum-double bed.

Earns: DJ

Paris Hilton has always been passionate about music. She began her career as a DJ in 2012 at a pop music festival in Brazil, followed by Ibiza the next year, winning the Breakthrough DJ Award. During her prime time, Hilton made $1 million per set as she toured the world in 2014, as noted by Cosmopolitan.

Spends: Exotic Vacations

Traveling around the world, Paris Hilton has seen the world through her extravagant and exotic locations. She has toured with Carter Reum to various places, including Greece while they were dating in the initial months, South Africa to celebrate their engagement, and spent a long honeymoon on the Bora Bora island.

Earns: Brand Endorsements

Paris Hilton gave a shot at modeling during her early 20s when she signed with Donald Trump’s modeling agency. She starred in various campaigns for Tommy Hilfiger, Guess, and Dior. After almost a decade, she returned to her modeling roots with Ye’s Yeezy Season 6 collection in 2018. Conrad Hotels also sponsored part of her honeymoon in the Maldives.

Spends: Important Life Events And Parties

Paris Hilton’s wedding was as lavish as her 21st birthday when she married Carter Reum in a beautiful wedding ceremony at a $65.1 million estate over three days. Hilton had six costume changes and wore a custom Oscar de la Renta gown for the ceremony, as reported by Harper’s Bazaar. It reportedly cost over $25,000.

Paris Hilton also made sizable money from her acting career, making minor movie appearances, and has spent millions on shopping sprees with her family. While being an heiress granted her security, Paris Hilton continued to grow her brand and established herself as a successful businesswoman and DJ.
