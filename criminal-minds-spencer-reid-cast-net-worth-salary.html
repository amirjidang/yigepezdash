'Criminal Minds': How Much Does Matthew Gray Gubler Make As Spencer Reid?

A hit television show is a quick way to pay the bills for any performer, and pilot season tends to be a busy time for this very reason. When a show like Outer Banks or Squid Game takes off, people can't stop buzzing about it, and this is great news for the actors, who will be compensated for their work.

For over 300 episodes, Matthew Gray Gubler played Spencer Reid on Criminal Minds, and he was able to leave a lasting impression on fans of the show. Thanks to Criminal Minds becoming a colossal hit, Gubler was able to make millions while playing Spencer Reid.

Let's take a closer look at the career of Matthew Gray Gubler and how much he was pulling down to play Spencer Reid on Criminal Minds.

Matthew Gray Gubler Has Been Acting Since The 2000s

At this point in his career, Matthew Gray Gubler is a popular actor who has found success on both the big and small screen. Gubler may be known best for playing Spencer Reid on Criminal Minds, but believe us when we say that the actor has done a lot more than most people realize.

On the big screen, Gubler's biggest success has come from voicing Simon Seville in the Alvin and the Chipmunks movies. Most people were shocked to learn that it was him, and he played Simon in all 4 movies. Gubler also had roles in RV, (500) Days of Summer, and Batman: Assault on Arkham, which gives him more than a few notable credits in the world of film acting.

On television, Gubler does not have nearly as many credits. He has appeared on a few other shows like Celebrity Ghost Stories, The Beauty Inside, and Dollface. As great as it is that Gubler has had tons of success, he is still known best for his time playing Spencer Reid on Criminal Minds.

He Was Amazing On ' Criminal Minds'

Back in 2005, Matthew Gray Gubler began his time playing Spencer Reid on Criminal Minds, and while seeing a procedural crime drama was nothing new for television audiences, this series was just what fans were looking for. In no time at all, it became a fixture on the small screen and a massive hit with fans.

Gubler was a fantastic pick for Spencer Reid, despite his lack of acting experience. For over 300 episodes, he was one of the best parts of the show itself, and the fans loved what Gubler brought to the table. He could have departed at any point, but Gubler genuinely loved playing the charcater, and he wound up sticking around for the long haul. When talking about playing the character, Gubler said, "Reid makes the most of it and so do I. It's fun and quite an honor. He's a Ph.D. in physics and mathematics and nobody knows what the third one is and I'm in no way scientifically minded. I think he's way more analytical than I am."

Landing the role of Spencer Reid changed everything for Matthew Gray Gubler, and because of the show's longstanding success, the actor was able to make a fortune while playing one of the show's most popular characters.

The Show Paid Him Well

So, how much did Matthew Gray Gubler make from playing Spencer Reid on Criminal Minds? Thanks to the show's success, the actor was able to pull down six figures during the show's most successful years.

Related: 'Criminal Minds' Actor Matthew Gray Gubler Might Be Single, But His Love Life Is Not Boring

Like other stars who land a role on a popular television show, Gubler wasn't making a premium at first, but over time, the show's popularity gave his salary a huge boost. Back in 2012, for instance, it was announced that Gubler was getting a bump in pay to start making $100,000 per episode, according to DigitalSpy. That was a nice uptick in pay, and it helped Gubler make millions while on the show.

According to TheList, Gubler would subsequently receive another pay increase, which brought things up to $150,000 per episode. Keep in mind that this pay does not reflect money that he has made from syndication, meaning that he certainly made a whole lot more for the episodes that have aired multiple times. TheList estimates that syndication bumped his pay to roughly $3.5 million annually.

Criminal Minds has come to an end, but Gubler has remained busy with a couple of projects. Should Gubler make his way onto another successful series, it will be interesting to see if he can top the salary that he made while playing Spencer Reid.
