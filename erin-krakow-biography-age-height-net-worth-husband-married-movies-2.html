Erin Krakow Career

Born in Philadelphia, Krakow graduated from the Flowing Wells High School and Arizona School for the Arts in Tucson, Arizona and later moved to West Palm Beach, Florida. She studied drama at the Juilliard School in New York City and started her acting career on stage, with roles in The Importance of Being Earnest, George the Fourth and Steel Magnolias. She had a recurring role from 2010 to 2012 of Specialist Tanya Gabriel in the Lifetime television drama series Army Wives, playing a medic in Afghanistan and later guest-starred on Castle.

Krakow started starring as the lead character in the 2014, Hallmark Channel drama series When Calls the Heart, together with Daniel Lissing. Krakow potrays teacher Elizabeth Thatcher, a role originated by Poppy Drayton in the movie that spawned the series. Krakow announced in April 2017, via the Hallmark Channel website that the show would return for a fifth season in February 2018. In December 2017, it premiered with a two-hour special.

In 2016, Krakow starred in Hallmark Movies & Mysteries film Finding Father Christmas. Adapted from the novels of Robin Jones Gunn, Krakow starred together with Niall Matter, as Miranda Chester, a woman who visits a small Vermont town at Christmas time, looking for clues as to the identity of her father. At the time of broadcast, the film became the most watched premier in the network’s history. Hallmark announced in May 2017 a sequel to the film. Filming for the sequel, entitled Engaging Father Christmas, took place in Vancouver in June 2017, with both Krakow and Matter reprising their roles. The film premiered in November 2017 on Hallmark Movies & Mysteries. Krakow started filming a third installment in the series, entitled Marrying Father Christmas in March 2018. The film will premiered in the USA on the Hallmark Movies & Mysteries channel on November 4, 2018, as part of their “Miracles of Christmas” seasonal programming.
