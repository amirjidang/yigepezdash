Early life & educational background of Craig Federighi

Apple software chief Craig Federighi announces iOS 17 new features

During the WWDC event, Craig Federighi, an executive at Apple, highlighted the company’s determined commitment to privacy while revealing new software and hardware updates.

He specifically discussed the advantages of AI and introduced features like “Check In” in iOS 17 that prioritize user satisfaction.

An example of one of these features is “Check In,” which offers iPhone users the option to select specific contacts who will receive automatic notifications when they arrive home after a night out, providing a sense of security.

In case the user fails to respond, Check In will send a message to the selected contacts, including precise details such as the user’s location, cell service status, iPhone battery level, and the timestamp of their last iPhone activity.

This information could prove invaluable in locating the user in the event of an unfortunate incident.

Apple is also enhancing Lockdown Mode, initially introduced in iOS 16, to safeguard users against persistent malware attacks.

The upcoming watchOS ten will include this feature, which will be strengthened by preventing the iPhone from connecting to 2G cellular networks and automatically joining insecure wireless networks.
