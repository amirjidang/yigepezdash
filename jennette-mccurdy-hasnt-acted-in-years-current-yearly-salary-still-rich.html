Jennette McCurdy Hasn't Acted In Years But Her Current Yearly Salary Proves She's Still Crazy Rich

Highlights

  • Former child star Jennette McCurdy has shifted her focus to writing, opening up about her toxic relationship with her late mother in her memoir.
  • Despite not acting anymore, McCurdy has a net worth of $3.5 million.
  • McCurdy's book "I'm Glad My Mom Died" is a bestseller.

If the first half of Jennette McCurdy's career was all about her charming role on iCarly, the past few years have been focused on her vulnerable writing. Ever since the release of her memoir "I'm Glad My Mom Died," McCurdy has opened up about the raw details of her toxic relationship with her late parent. Releasing this book was a huge deal professionally and personally for the former child star. While McCurdy turned down an extra $300,000 when she got her book deal, she still made a fortune. She continues to talk about her mother on her podcast and in interviews.

Considering some of Jennette McCurdy's acting experiences, like feeling anxiety about eating food on Sam & Cat, it makes sense that she decided to stop acting a few years ago. But even though she hasn't been on a movie or TV set in a long time, McCurdy is still earning an impressive amount of money.

What Is Jennette McCurdy's Net Worth And How Does She Make Money?

Jennette McCurdy has a $3.5 million net worth, according to Celebrity Net Worth, which is fairly high given the fact that she doesn't act anymore. While the majority of McCurdy's net worth seems to have come from the salary that she earned when acting, her current main source of income seems to be her book deals.

In 2022, Jennette McCurdy talked about money in an interview on The Financial Diet. When the subject of residuals came up, she said:

"I would make an episodic salary. OK. And usually, I'd get some sort of a bonus if we got picked up for another season. However-- and I think people might be surprised to learn this-- zero residuals."

She continued, "So for network television, you make residuals when an episode airs any time, I believe, after the first airing and every time after that. And if it's picked up from, like, a Friends-- the Friends series might be picked up on TBS, and then they're airing reruns on TBS, and also on whatever other network. But for Nickelodeon, you do not see residuals."

Based on the fact that stars often sign book deals for millions, as Buzzfeed noted, she likely did very well. Since her book has been consistently selling, she must be earning royalties.

While she has a comfortable net worth, Jennette McCurdy wants to keep working, and it's moving to see how well her memoir has been received. After being controlled by her mother, she is finally doing what she wants, and that means embarking on a successful writing career.

Why Did Jennette McCurdy Quit Acting?

In 2021, Jennette McCurdy talked about stopping acting in an interview on one of her podcasts, Empty Inside. According to Entertainment Weekly, she mentioned that her mother made her go on auditions and become part of Hollywood. She felt overwhelmed by the fact that she had to keep acting even though she wasn't actually interested. It sounds like a terrible experience.

McCurdy said:

"I quit a few years ago because I initially didn't want to do it. My mom put me in it when I was 6 and by sort of age, I guess, 10 or 11, I was the main financial support for my family."

Since McCurdy gave such a good performance in iCarly and Sam & Cat, fans might be surprised to learn that she didn't find much satisfaction in any of the characters that she portrayed.

McCurdy explained, "I feel so unfulfilled by the roles that I played. I imagine there's a very different experience to be had with acting if you're proud of your roles, and if you feel fulfilled by them."

Although Jennette McCurdy hasn't acted since 2018, it's nice to see that she is earning money through other artistic avenues.
